Intelligent Wireless Sensing Computing Group

Northwestern Polytechnical University, School of Computer Science, Professor Yang Bo's group - Intelligent Wireless Communication Computing Research Group, the main research areas around the ubiquitous swarm intelligence network, wireless sensing, resource allocation, cloud computing, intelligent reflection surface direction, is committed to creating an international impact of communication and artificial intelligence research.

Prof Yang Bo

Dr Yang Bo has conducted in-depth research in recent years on the frontiers of ubiquitous swarm intelligence networks, wireless sensing, resource allocation, cloud-edge computing, intelligent reflective surfaces, etc., and was awarded the first National Natural Science Foundation of China's Outstanding Young Scientist Fund (Overseas).He has been engaged in post-doctoral research in the United States and Singapore successively from 2017 to 2022, and has participated in a number of major international research projects and technological research projects (including the US Department of Defense's military big data network research project and Singapore A*STAR engineering research project, etc.). During this period, he has participated in many major international research projects and technological research projects (including the US Department of Defense's military big data network research project and Singapore's A*STAR engineering research project, etc.), and has been granted 11 domestic and foreign invention patents (including 8 Chinese patents, 2 US patents, and 1 European patent, of which 7 patents have been included in Huawei's reserve patents for the next-generation IEEE 802.11 standard) and 1 proposal for IEEE 802.11 standard (the proposal for the standard is a reserve patent for the next generation IEEE 802.11 standard). In April 2022, he joined the School of Computer Science of Northwestern Polytechnical University as a professor and a doctoral director.
